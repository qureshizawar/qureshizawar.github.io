Details

I started with the style transfer model described in the Real-Time Style Transfer paper, which proved too slow/computationally expensive for low power and edge devices.

To bring down the computational cost, I first replaced the convolutional layers with separable filters (as in MobileNet achitectures). To further bring down the cost and tune the models; model achitecture search and pruning (as described in this paper) was employed. Finally, all the layers in the models that could be fused were fused to give the final optimised model.

This allowed for significant reduction in model parameters (as shown in the table below) making realtime inference on low power devices feasible. All models were trained and optimised in Pytorch and then converted for deployment on TensorFlow.js.

Model Prams. FLOPs size size(quantised)
Original ~1.68M ~6.39G @ 384x216 6.4MB 1.6MB
Separable ~211.07K ~335M @ 384x216 886KB 221KB
Optimised ~22.2K ~185M @ 384x216 87KB 21.75KB
